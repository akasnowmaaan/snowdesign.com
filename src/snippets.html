    • Design principles

    The problem. We understood what our customers were asking for, but we were stuck. This was a clean slate, we had immense new capabilities, everyone wanted to do the right thing, but we didn’t have clear direction.

    Kick-off. So, with Mathias' help, I drafted set of design principles, driven by a combination of our internal philosophy, business goals, and resource realities. We distributed them framed with 'here’s a notion of where we think we need to go, everyone tear it to shreds and see if it holds up.'

    Feedback. We then got feedback in every way we could - we made sure everyone had a voice in shaping them. We also made it abundantly clear we needed frank critique; this document was supposed to become a rallying cry.

    Results. We did the right thing - the design principles were greeted with a sigh of relief. Suggested edits were small, and easily incorporated. The chaos immediately stopped and teams started working with a renewed sense of focus.

    New feature: persistent draft

    We figured out a way to automatically save the current state of an in-progress ad, so if a customer comes back to Tradera days later, it will be exactly how they left it. I built the interface to handle the unintended consequences, such as losing the ability to close the browser tab to start over.

    Result. This feature is a huge delighter and source of comfort. Customers now felt much less stress about losing their work while composing a detailed listing. It remains a key differentiator of Tradera's selling experience.
